Why should I use an infrared sauna?

Read Post

An infrared sauna is a type of sauna that uses infrared heaters to emit far-infrared rays. These rays are not visible and can’t be felt, but they penetrate the skin’s surface and warm up the body from within. Infrared radiation has been found to increase blood circulation and metabolism by heating tissue just beneath the skin’s surface, thereby increasing fat loss. The increased blood flow also helps remove toxins from tissues while providing greater oxygenation for muscles. Increased metabolism means accelerated weight loss, which makes it easier to maintain your desired weight after you’ve lost it. The deep warmth created in an infrared sauna also relieves pain such as arthritis, muscle spasms or stiffness, chronic low back pain and sciatica (nerve pain), and eases stress-related tension, fatigue, headaches, minor depression, and other mood problems.

The warmth stimulates sweat glands, which speed up the elimination of toxins through the skin. Sweating is also known to relax muscles, reduce blood pressure and heart rate, improve metabolism and circulation, clear congested lungs or sinuses due to colds or allergies, kill germs that cause infections – even acne – cleanse pores of dirt and oil buildup, open clogged pores for relief from blackheads, improve absorption of ointments used in the treatment of eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis, etc., soothe skin after excessive sun exposure caused by tanning or strenuous exercise, help wounds heal faster by removing dead tissue, stimulate nerve endings for greater relaxation, improve joint mobility and strengthen brittle nails.

Infrared saunas are a great way to detox

Infrared saunas can help you get rid of toxins in your body, improve circulation, and manage pain due to inflammation. They also increase the rate at which your skin can heal itself by boosting collagen production and reducing dryness. Plus, they’re good for your heart! The heat makes it easier for blood vessels near the surface of the skin to dilate (or “open up”), which brings more oxygenated blood into the cells around them; this helps lower cholesterol levels and reduces high blood pressure. All that sounds pretty awesome—so what’s not to love?

Well, infrared saunas do have their downsides too: they require electricity or gas heating elements to function, which means they can be a drain on your resources. And because you sit so close to the heater in an infrared sauna, it’s easy for those toxins and byproducts that would otherwise be flushed out of your body instead to end up trapped against your skin, leading to an even higher risk of irritation such as rashes and sunburn (and not much relief from pain, if we’re being honest). Plus, studies have shown that too much exposure to infrared radiation can increase cancer risk.
